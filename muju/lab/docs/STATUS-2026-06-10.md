## Headline findings

1. **Rush is over-nerfed against defense, dominant against greed** (E6).
   Mass-fire_1 wins **14.2%** [12.0–16.8] vs the scripted AntiRush and
   **6.7%** vs the hard AI — the E-2 target band is 35–55% — while beating
   pure-economy Expand 97.2%. The game is currently "answer-or-die": any
   prepared defense walls rush out entirely; only naive greed loses to it.
2. **The element graph is what makes defense work** (E7). Remove the
   water/shadow→fire/lightning −1 back-edge and AntiRush collapses from 88%
   to 2–4%. The incumbent Double-Thick Triangle stays (ruling E-1); rush-band
   repair must use stat/cost levers, not the graph.
3. **±1 ATK is a sledgehammer** (G4): a global +1 swings mirrors ~45 points
   because it flips one-shot thresholds across the T1 roster. Fine balance
   lives in costs and build times, not combat stats.
4. **Trap lines** (E4): Lightning loses every mono-line matchup except vs
   plant (DEF 1 everywhere + MINE 0 early). Within pairs, water dominates
   shadow 99% and metal dominates plant 96%.
5. **No first-player advantage** (E3: 48–53% across mirrors); first blood
   does not predict winning.
6. **Two real engine bugs found by the lab instrument**: D13 (same-millisecond
   ID collisions teleported units in real games — fixed + regression-tested)
   and **D14 (new)**: the AI's belief-determinized plans emit MOVE/ATTACKs
   that are illegal in the real state, and `ai/simulate` applies them without
   validation (ghost attacks burn actions; `applyMove` has no occupancy
   guard). 0.3–3.5 emissions/game depending on matchup.
