## The Podium

### 🥇 #1 — TESSER `[S2]` *(user seed)*

**TESSER**

- **Pitch**: A dueling tactics game where every piece is the same substance at different dimensionalities. Fold a tower into a spear, unfold a spear across time — hypervolume is always conserved.

- **Core loop**: Each turn: move one piece, fold one. Folding redistributes fixed measure — eight units is a piercing 1×8 line (1D), a ground-holding 2×4 slab (2D), a height-8 tower that blocks lanes (3D), or a worldline eight turns thin (4D) that vanishes and secretly re-materializes on a committed future turn. Combat is cross-sectioning: a slab shaves a plane off a tower. Zero the enemy's total measure.

- **Signature mechanic**: Conservation of measure — one number, four geometries — plus abilities keyed per-dimension, so every shape is four pieces in one. And 4D is hidden information natively: opponents see ghost slices, never your committed re-entry turn.

- **Platform exploitation**: ai — 4D concealment is re-determinized ISMCTS's home turf (cross-sections stay a deterministic public channel); difficulty = budget. lab — conservation as an invariant abort; every pack certified into 45–55% bands. content — zod shape schemas; campaign puzzles verified solvable/notPreSolved. llm — monthly shapes coded via implement-jobs, shipping only past verifier + lab, so the discovery space never closes. core/ui — folds are commit points; tap-select-confirm portrait play.

- **Lineage & leap**: Succeeds Ninja Tanks' indistinguishable decoys, Muju's hidden-queue honest AI, MMS's measure-as-life-bar attrition. The leap: dimensionality itself as the mutable stat — discrete fold rules, no physics, no netcode; V1 is six shapes, a taught campaign, one ISMCTS opponent.

- **Session shape**: 12–18-turn duels, 10–20 minutes, one-thumb portrait; every fold writes the save envelope, so mid-thought interruption costs nothing.

#### Deep-dive treatment (post-tournament, answering the judges' critiques)


**Refined pitch**
A symmetric dueling tactics game where every piece is conserved hypervolume worn as geometry: fold a tower into a spear, unfold a spear across time, never gain or lose an ounce. The tournament's "16-case interaction matrix" dissolves into one law — combat is projection, damage is overlap.

**The loop, concretely**
Turn 7. You hold 22 measure across three pieces (measure lives **per-piece**; folding reshapes a piece but never transfers between pieces — transfer is a future ability, not a free move). Move: advance your 2×4 slab one cell toward the enemy's height-6 tower. Fold: your 1×8 spear becomes a 2×2×2 cube to plug a lane — a commit point, save envelope written. Attack resolves by the single cross-section law: *the attacker projects onto the defender's occupied cells; damage = the overlap's measure, capped by the attacker's exposed face.* Slab meets tower on one shared cell at height 2 → shaves 2; tower is now height 4. One formula, sixteen consequences — no lookup table to design or teach.

**V1 scope cut**
Ships: 1D/2D/3D folding only, four shapes, **zero per-dimension abilities** (pure geometry first), a 12-mission taught campaign, one AI, portrait UI. Waits: 4D worldlines (campaign Act 2, first post-V1 patch), shapes five and six, abilities, monthly LLM shape drops, PvP. If the bare three-dimension fold economy isn't fun, abilities were makeup.

**Kit-by-kit build plan**
- **core**: GameDef with folds as commit points; every fold writes the save envelope — interruption costs nothing.
- **lab**: conservation-of-measure as a literal invariant abort; probe bots (TowerTurtle, SpearRush, NeverFold) must land shape packs in 45–55% Wilson bands.
- **ai**: V1 without 4D is perfect-information → plain minimax with optionality eval; ISMCTS over the tiny (turn × cell) particle space arrives with the 4D patch.
- **content**: zod shape schemas; campaign puzzles machine-verified solvable/notPreSolved.
- **llm**: unused in V1, deliberately — implement-jobs shape drops are the never-closing discovery engine, but wiring generation before the base economy is lab-proven repeats LUMENGRID's failure mode.
- **ui**: tap-select-confirm; a tower renders as footprint + height numeral + isometric stub — paper-prototyped for legibility before any other code.

**Biggest risk & mitigation**
That dimensionality collapses into four stances wearing geometry flavor. Mitigation: the projection law makes dimension mechanically load-bearing (exposure-vs-footprint is a geometric tradeoff, not a stat skin), and lab must show every dimension winning ≥30% of probe-bot matchups before any content work starts.

**First playtest question**
Shown a tower, a slab, and a spear, can a new player predict one attack's damage — and do they voluntarily fold by turn 5?

### 🥈 #2 — DRY RUN `[N5]` *(ideated (platform-maximalist lens))*

**DRY RUN** — you are not doing the heist; you are rehearsing it in your head.
- **Pitch**: Rewind freely through a guarded building — until you do something the guards would remember, and the plan hardens behind you.
- **Core loop**: Study a blueprint, ghost through patrols step by step, undoing at will. Noisy actions — breaking a lock, crossing a camera — are commit points that freeze everything before them and spawn a live alert state. Extract the score; unspent rewinds multiply it.
- **Signature mechanic**: Diegetic undo. @deev/core's commit-point-aware undo is the central verb, the scarce resource, and the score multiplier — and hidden patrol variants re-determinize at each commit, so you cannot rewind-scout the truth.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/core's undo/commit machinery IS the gameplay, and transcripts prove every clear. @deev/content blueprint packs: zod schemas plus a verifier proving each is solvable-with-≤K-commits and notPreSolved (no free walk-ins). @deev/ai ISMCTS guards plan over beliefs about which route you committed to. @deev/lab calibrates star ratings from clear-rate and commit-count distributions. @deev/llm structuredCall generates blueprint variants from constraint prompts, verifier-gated before admission.
- **Lineage & leap**: Succeeds LUMENGRID's machine-verified puzzles and MMS's honest telegraphs; the leap is turning save/undo infrastructure — the most invisible part of the platform — into the fantasy itself.
- **Session shape**: One blueprint runs 8–15 minutes, and every commit point is a safe resume point by construction; ideal for interrupted mobile play.

#### Deep-dive treatment (post-tournament, answering the judges' critiques)


**Refined pitch**: You rehearse a heist in your head: rewinding is *free and unlimited*, but noisy actions are **commits** that harden the plan behind you. Score is loot times commit efficiency against par — the tension is Balatro-style *spending* (each extra commit unlocks richer loot rooms but risks blowing par), never hoarding. The unused-consumable trap is gone: the verb you ration is commit, and the game constantly tempts you to spend one more.

**The loop, concretely**: You open a bank blueprint showing the vault, three loot tiers, and — face-up — the guard "intel deck": three possible patrol variants per guard, all visible. You ghost forward, scrub back, ghost again; the pre-commit world is fully deterministic and honestly telegraphed (MMS lesson: one function drives display and execution). You break the stairwell lock — commit #1. The intel deck collapses: one variant per guard is dealt live, everything before the lock freezes, alert timers start. You probe the new truth, rewind *to the commit*, try again. Commit #2 (camera dash) buys the gem annex. Par is 3; you bank out at 2 with mid-tier loot, or gamble a third for the crown. Extract; the transcript proves the clear. 8–15 minutes; every commit is a save point.

**V1 scope cut**: Ships: one hand-authored 12-blueprint pack, 2 guard archetypes, 3 patrol variants each, commit/rewind/extract, par scoring. Waits: LLM-generated packs, gadgets (glass cutter, decoy radio — the compounding-synergy layer that answers the treadmill critique), daily-seed mode, campaign meta.

**Kit-by-kit build plan**:
- **core**: commit-point-aware undo IS the game verb; transcripts + replay convergence prove clears; save envelopes at commits.
- **lab**: greedy vs BFS-optimal bots calibrate par and star curves from clear-rate/commit-count distributions with Wilson CIs.
- **ai**: unused at runtime — *justified*: stealth demands legible guards (the honest-telegraph lesson); adversarial ISMCTS guards were the one bolted-on claim, so they're cut.
- **content**: zod blueprint schemas + the verifier: pre-alert movement is deterministic per variant, so solvable-under-par/notPreSolved is BFS over (position, time, commits) per variant — the LUMENGRID pattern, a checker not a planner.
- **llm**: post-V1 only — structuredCall generates blueprints offline, admitted only if they pass the verifier *plus* interest metrics (solution uniqueness, near-miss density); zero runtime calls.
- **ui**: mobile shell, tap-select-then-confirm for step vs commit, localStorage saves, deterministic toasts on collapse.

**Biggest risk & mitigation**: Intel-collapse feels like cheating. Mitigation: variants are face-up before commit — you're planning against a known distribution, and the collapse animation deals the card you already saw.

**First playtest question**: When par is 3, do players voluntarily spend the 3rd commit chasing bonus loot — or still bank at 2?

### 🥉 #3 — HOARDFALL `[S8]` *(user seed)*

**HOARDFALL** — Risk shrunk to a knife-fight, where the treasure is the war.

- **Pitch**: A 16-province elemental map where mana is banked *in the land itself* — every hoard you stockpile is loot your invader is coming for.
- **Core loop**: Provinces bank mana of their element each turn. You cast one spell from a shared face-up river, paying from stores you control — or *seal* a river card face-down for later (opponents saw the pool, not your pick). March armies; battles resolve deterministically, spell-modified, no dice. Conquer a province, seize its unspent hoard.
- **Signature mechanic**: Mana lives on the map, not in a pool. Turtling literally arms your enemy: rich hoards paint targets, so the economy self-balances aggression and losing provinces sheds the bounty on your head. No snowball, no dead turns.
- **Platform exploitation**: Sealed spells give re-determinized ISMCTS genuine particle beliefs (@deev/ai); pure deterministic GameDef with envelope saves (@deev/core); spells are zod-schema'd effect-ASTs, LLM-forged via implement-jobs, verifier-gated, then lab-proven balanced with Wilson CIs before any pack ships (@deev/content, @deev/llm, @deev/lab); tap-province-confirm mobile shell (@deev/ui).
- **Lineage & leap**: Muju's territory-and-element bones, FORGE's contested shared market, Lution's never-closing content space — but mana-as-loot inverts the hoarding instinct; nothing in the catalog punishes wealth with warfare.
- **Session shape**: One scenario map, ~20 turns, 15–25 minutes vs AI; commit-point undo and saves make it pocketable mid-siege.

#### Deep-dive treatment (post-tournament, answering the judges' critiques)


**Refined pitch.** A 16-province knife-fight where mana is banked in the land and every hoard is a bounty on its owner's head. The refined signature is **spoils spillage**: conquest never pays in full — seized hoards scatter to neighboring provinces, so victory redistributes wealth outward instead of compounding it. That one rule is the anti-snowball engine, not a slogan.

**The loop, concretely.** Turn 12. Your three fire provinces each bank 1 fire mana; your border hoard hits 7 — dangerously rich. The river shows five face-up spells. You seal *Emberfall* face-down (your third seal this game; the AI's belief over your stash is now 5×4×5 joint particles, not a guessable handful). You march two armies into the enemy's water province. The battle ledger shows one line pre-commit: `ATK 6 − DEF 4 + seals? = win by 2 (or less)` — same function drives preview and resolution. You confirm; you win, but only 4 of its 7 banked mana enters your treasury: 3 spill to adjacent provinces, one of them enemy-held. The conquered province is *razed* — banks nothing for 2 turns. You end turn richer, more exposed, and no closer to runaway.

**V1 scope cut.** Ships: 2-player vs AI, seeded procedural province-element maps (killing the solved-map risk without spell packs), 24 hand-tuned spells, spillage + razing, full save/undo. Waits: LLM spell-forge (V1.1, once the executor is proven on the hand-tuned set), multiplayer, campaign framing.

**Kit-by-kit build plan.**
- **core**: pure deterministic GameDef; commit-point undo mid-siege; envelope saves.
- **lab**: sweeps seizure-fraction × spillage × raze-duration; invariant-abort if first-conqueror win rate exits 45–55% — "no snowball" gets falsified before UI work.
- **ai**: ISMCTS with particles over *stacked* seals (up to 3 persist across turns), restoring a genuine belief space.
- **content**: zod province/spell schemas; map generator gated by `notPreSolved`/`survivable` verifiers.
- **llm**: unused in V1 — justified: the effect-AST executor ships hand-fed, so the Lution-precedented forge bolts on later without touching the kernel.
- **ui**: tap-province-confirm shell; single-line battle ledger (no spreadsheet-squinting).

**Biggest risk & mitigation.** The SF2 critique: conqueror takes land *and* treasury — Risk runaway relabeled. Mitigation is structural (spillage + razing make conquest tempo-positive but economy-neutral) *and* empirical: the lab invariant above is a ship-blocker, not a hope.

**First playtest question.** After seizing your first rich hoard, did you feel *stronger* — or *hunted*? If the answer is only "stronger," the signature has failed.

### 4th #4 — FALSE MANIFEST `[N1]` *(ideated (platform-maximalist lens))*

**FALSE MANIFEST** — an asymmetric customs duel.
- **Pitch**: One player smuggles under a legally binding cargo manifest; the other spends a scarce inspection budget to catch the lie. The declaration is public and permanent; the cargo is not.
- **Core loop**: The smuggler loads a hold from a contract deck, files a manifest, and writes a one-line cover story. The inspector reads noisy dock rumors, watches a suspicion meter, and chooses which crates to open — every opened crate re-determinizes what the rest could hold. Five voyages, then scoring.
- **Signature mechanic**: The cover story is mechanically load-bearing. makeJudge scores its plausibility against the manifest and visible dock evidence, and that score sets the inspector's per-crate inspection cost. Lying well is literally cheaper.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/ai ISMCTS plays both seats over cargo-particle beliefs. @deev/llm makeJudge prices stories with a frozen rubric; structuredCall generates contract decks. @deev/content schemas plus an explicit-seeds verifier prove every contract is both smuggle-able and catchable (notPreSolved in both directions). @deev/lab proves 45–55% seat balance across inspection budgets with Wilson CIs. @deev/core caches judge rulings in the transcript so replays converge byte-for-byte.
- **Lineage & leap**: Succeeds Ninja Tanks' decoy duality and Muju's hidden-queue honesty; the leap is a natural-language artifact priced by a judge inside an otherwise fully mechanical game.
- **Session shape**: One voyage per round, ~15 minutes total; each filed manifest is a commit point and safe resume; mobile portrait, tap-crate-then-confirm.

#### Deep-dive treatment (post-tournament, answering the judges' critiques)


**Refined pitch**
An asymmetric customs duel where you smuggle under a legally binding manifest and a one-line cover story that a frozen judge literally prices — lying well makes inspection expensive. V1 flips the seats decisively: the human is always the smuggler, and the AI inspector is pure ISMCTS over cargo particles. No bot ever authors prose, so the advertised AI is the AI that ships.

**The loop, concretely**
Voyage 3 of 5. You draw a contract: deliver 4 silk, 2 contraband powder, bonus for undeclared relics. You load nine crates, file a manifest ("9 crates: silk, dye, ledger paper") — a commit point, safe to pocket the phone. You write the cover: "Dye lots ride with the silk so the monsoon damp doesn't streak them." The judge scores it 78 against the manifest *and this voyage's generated dock evidence* (a dye shortage rumor is live — lucky). Inspector's per-crate cost jumps from 2 to 3 against a budget of 7. The ISMCTS inspector opens crate 4 — silk, as declared — re-determinizes its particle cloud, opens crate 7, finds powder. Partial bust: you forfeit that line's value, suspicion meter rises into voyage 4. Scoring after five voyages.

**V1 scope cut**
Ships: single-player smuggler-seat vs ISMCTS inspector, one verifier-gated contract deck, five voyages, one judge call per voyage. Waits: AI smuggler seat (needs fragment-composition research), PvP, additional decks, rubric v2.

**Kit-by-kit build plan**
- **core**: GameDef with manifest-filing commit points; judge rulings cached in transcript keyed by (story, manifest, evidence) for byte-identical replays.
- **lab**: story score becomes a *swept input parameter* (0/25/50/75/100) — proves 45–55% seat balance with Wilson CIs across inspection budgets at every score band, zero LLM calls; the judge only needs to land scores in-distribution, verified separately against a 200-story cached calibration corpus in CI.
- **ai**: ISMCTS inspector over cargo-particle beliefs with per-crate re-determinization; difficulty = particle count.
- **content**: zod contract schemas; explicit-seeds verifier proving every contract both smuggle-able and catchable.
- **llm**: makeJudge (frozen rubric, evidence-conditioned) prices stories; structuredCall generates decks offline. Zero-key CI via the calibration corpus.
- **ui**: mobile portrait shell, tap-crate-then-confirm, suspicion meter, localStorage saves at manifest commits.

**Biggest risk & mitigation**
Judge-golfing: a solved rubric collapses discovery into incantation. Mitigation is mechanical, not rubric patches — scores are conditioned on per-voyage generated evidence (a memorized story can't fit arbitrary docks), and the *dockmaster's memory* makes filed stories public record: textual similarity to your own past stories raises base suspicion. Reuse is taxed in-fiction, permanently.

**First playtest question**
After five voyages, do players craft a new story each time — or repeat their best one and eat the suspicion tax?

## The Bracket

### Quarterfinal 1: **FALSE MANIFEST** `[N1]` vs **QUILL & QUARRY** `[S5]`

**Winner: FALSE MANIFEST** (clear). QUILL & QUARRY eliminated.

> Attacking both: N1's real wounds are discovery closure (a frozen rubric can be metagamed into prose-vs-judge exploit farming; new contract decks add instances, not mechanics) and a quiet crack in its lab story — the smuggler's cover story is NL, so bot-vs-bot runs need stubbed/cached stories, slightly weakening the "no LLM in the loop" boast. But those wounds are bounded and mitigable. S5's wound is at the core: critique-as-combat requires an LLM judge to reliably map free-text literary claims onto a beat graph — an unmeasured research problem (lab is admittedly judge-stubbed), and if the judge wobbles, the entire combat system feels arbitrary. Stacked on hybrid verbs, solvability verification, prose rendering, a persistent Ledger, and implement-jobs, that's months of icebergs against a hard "V1 via spec-driven subagents in weeks" constraint. Decisive factor: feasibility plus falsifiability of the signature mechanic. S5's best surviving virtue: the Cliché Ledger's permanent combo-devaluation — a genuinely never-closing discovery engine worth harvesting later.

### Quarterfinal 2: **DRY RUN** `[N5]` vs **WARDLIGHT** `[N3]`

**Winner: DRY RUN** (clear). WARDLIGHT eliminated.

> Decisive factor: which wounds are structural. N3's are — its creature is scripted and non-adversarial, so @deev/ai's actual planners and core's undo machinery sit idle (the "deepest exploitation" claim is inflated to particle visualization), and its decision layer is thin: the filter performs the cognition while the player's greedy sensor placement plausibly approximates optimal play, leaving longevity to a species treadmill whose interestingness no verifier can gate. N5's wounds are tuning problems: the alleged verifier iceberg mostly dissolves (pre-alert stealth is deterministic per patrol variant — BFS over position/time/commits, squarely the LUMENGRID solvable/notPreSolved pattern @deev/content already ships), and the rewind-economy "tax" is exactly the Balatro-style spend tension this player profile rewards. N5's real risk — re-determinization reading as cheating — is a communication/framing problem, not architecture. N3's best surviving virtue: the genuinely simplest V1 in the field, and it fills the portfolio's stated cozy family gap.

### Quarterfinal 3: **PRISMBREAK** `[S6]` vs **TESSER** `[S2]`

**Winner: TESSER** (clear). PRISMBREAK eliminated.

> Decisive factor: S6's fun and feasibility cases both hang on one unproven artifact — a verifier proving ≥2 non-dominant routes through a state space coupling polyomino locomotion, body-refracted optics, sentinel beliefs, and an underspecified social channel. LUMENGRID verified static grids; route-bots that solve immersive-sim rooms are research, not a @deev/lab call. Since PRISMBREAK's rooms are consumable, discovery depth depends entirely on LLM districts clearing that same overloaded gate — one miracle, two load-bearing claims. TESSER's wounds are survivable: its 24-role space reads more like duel mastery than Balatro discovery, and 4D on a phone is a real teaching iceberg, but ghost slices are designed telegraphs, Muju proved hidden-info-vs-honest-AI is portfolio-loved, and a symmetric zero-physics duel is exactly what lab/ai/core can actually certify. S6's best surviving virtue: body-as-circuit is the genuinely original unification, and it targets the dossier's confirmed verifier gap.

### Quarterfinal 4: **HOARDFALL** `[S8]` vs **HEARTWOOD** `[S4]`

**Winner: HOARDFALL** (clear). HEARTWOOD eliminated.

> Decisive factor: failure-mode repairability. Both concepts have real flaws, but HOARDFALL's worst ones — the hoard-seizure snowball and thin belief space — are measurable and tunable with the platform itself (seizure fractions and pacing are lab-sweepable parameters; spell packs refresh the solved-map risk). HEARTWOOD's worst flaw sits at its center and is invisible to the verification stack: at 2 years/second, ring-commit agency is either frantic or spectator-sparse, the pitch never resolves which, and no headless lab run can prove "watching centuries obey" feels authored rather than watched. Its "V1 in weeks" claim hides a legibility/pacing tuning treadmill, and ISMCTS-over-continuous-growth is kit usage by label. HEARTWOOD's surviving virtues are genuine: rings-as-transcript is the most elegant kit-native idea in either concept, and it fills the cozy/non-combat portfolio gap — worth salvaging as a future concept once the agency loop is actually designed.

### Semifinal 1: **FALSE MANIFEST** `[N1]` vs **DRY RUN** `[N5]`

**Winner: DRY RUN** (clear). FALSE MANIFEST eliminated.

> Attacking both: N1's signature mechanic fails my verification test. The entire economy hinges on makeJudge pricing free-text at runtime — but @deev/lab bots cannot author cover stories, so the advertised 45–55% Wilson-CI balance proof necessarily validates templated stories, not the shipped game; the frozen rubric means discovered prose exploits are permanent; and the AI smuggler seat puts an LLM call in every voyage, breaking the zero-key/cheap-CI story. The one load-bearing beam is the one component the platform cannot verify, balance, or patch. N5's weaknesses are real but survivable: the ≤K-commits verifier against ISMCTS guards is the hidden iceberg (a per-blueprint adversarial search, not a zod check), and single-player puzzle packs risk a treadmill — but LUMENGRID precedent, verifier-gated generation, and a fully deterministic, netcode-free, runtime-LLM-free spec make it shippable by Sonnet subagents in weeks. N1's surviving virtue: hidden cargo with per-crate re-determinization is the round's cleanest ISMCTS showcase, and a judge-priced lie is genuinely original.

### Semifinal 2: **TESSER** `[S2]` vs **HOARDFALL** `[S8]`

**Winner: TESSER** (clear). HOARDFALL eliminated.

> HOARDFALL fails my attack at its center: "turtling arms your enemy" does not prevent snowballing — the conqueror seizes territory AND treasury, the classic Risk runaway relabeled as a feature, and the whole signature collapses to Muju-plus-FORGE-with-spells if lab disproves the slogan. Its hidden information (one sealed card from a river both players saw) is an enumerable handful of particles — ISMCTS in name, minimax in kind. TESSER takes real hits — the 4D fold is a tempo-vs-dominance dilemma, and ghost-slice readability on a phone is untested — but both are tunable inside a closed, dice-free kernel: per-dimension abilities and re-entry effects are levers, and even a niche 4D leaves a live 1D/2D/3D fold economy. Its conservation invariant is a literal lab invariant-abort; its kit usage is structural. Decisive factor: which signature survives falsification. HOARDFALL's surviving virtue: the most spec-able, genuinely weeks-shippable V1 kernel in the field.

### Grand Final: **DRY RUN** `[N5]` vs **TESSER** `[S2]`

**Winner: TESSER** (narrow). DRY RUN eliminated.

> Under attack, N5's wound is the one the brief calls fatal: the discovery phase. Its depth is consumed per blueprint — solve-once puzzles whose longevity depends on LLM-generated levels verified only for solvability, never interest (LUMENGRID's exact failure mode) — and the score multiplier institutionalizes avoiding the signature verb, so optimal play rations the fantasy. The re-determinization defense is thematically coherent but converts "rehearsal" into distribution-learning, thinner than advertised, and @deev/ai only decorates the post-commit slice. S2's icebergs are real — a 24-identity tuning surface and the 4D teaching burden — but they are precisely what @deev/lab was born (via Muju) to referee, V1 is scoped to six shapes versus one AI, and shapes-are-rules yields compounding, never-closing synergy discovery: the Balatro-grade product the player profile demands, with no content treadmill. N5's best surviving virtue: the cleanest, most honestly spec-able V1 in the field — shippable in weeks.

### Bronze Match: **FALSE MANIFEST** `[N1]` vs **HOARDFALL** `[S8]`

**Winner: HOARDFALL** (clear). FALSE MANIFEST eliminated.

> Decisive factor: where each concept's fatal risk lives. HOARDFALL's worst wounds — familiar Muju/FORGE bones, a spell-pack balance treadmill — are deferrable: V1 ships as a bounded, deterministic, fully spec-able state machine with a hand-tuned spell set, and the effect-AST/implement-jobs pipeline is precedented by shipped Lution, not speculative. FALSE MANIFEST's fatal risk sits inside its signature mechanic: a frozen prose rubric is an optimization target, so discovery depth collapses into keyword incantation the moment a player solves the judge — and no platform tool can disprove this, since lab balance is measured over bot-written stories, a proxy that says nothing about human-prose exploitation. Cached rulings fix replays, not fairness, and the AI smuggler seat needs live generation each voyage. N1's best surviving virtue: it genuinely cashes the Palimpsest thread — a language artifact as a mechanically priced object is the portfolio's most original unbuilt idea, with an admirably tiny GameDef.

## Seeding Committee Notes

How the top 8 were chosen and seeded from the pool results:

- **#1 FALSE MANIFEST** `[N1]` — Highest calibrated score (80) and the only concept where all six kits are load-bearing rather than listed: judge-priced cover stories, both-directions verifier, cached-ruling determinism. Its wounds (template smuggler bot, judge-in-loop lab cost) are engineering-shaped, not concept-shaped — the safest strong bet in the field.
- **#2 PRISMBREAK** `[S6]` — Only Pool 1 concept surviving all four assassins: body-as-circuit fuses locomotion and hacking into one deep combinatorial object, deterministic and verifier-friendly with the best session-shape fit. Flaws are trimmable scope (drop the immersive-sim/social pillar), not structural.
- **#3 HOARDFALL** `[S8]` — Pool 3 winner whose platform story 'needed no charity' — sealed spells give ISMCTS a genuine hidden channel, effect-AST spells reuse the proven Lution pipeline, and it is the most V1-scopable entry submitted. Its only real wound is novelty-safety (a confident remix), which costs a crown but not a bracket slot.
- **#4 DRY RUN** `[N5]` — Most platform-native concept in Pool 2: commit-point undo IS the verb, re-determinization closes the rewind-scouting exploit, and every commit is a resume point — the cleanest interruptibility story anywhere. Its contradiction (free rewinds vs scored hoarding) is fixable in a spec pass; the authored-content treadmill is the ceiling risk.
- **#5 WARDLIGHT** `[N3]` — Pool 5 winner: belief-as-instrument is a genuine inversion of the portfolio's ISMCTS DNA with the cleanest no-iceberg V1 in its pool, and it owns the requested cozy lane while staying mechanically dense. Seeded below S8/N5 because it uses only the belief half of @deev/ai, keeps llm compile-time-only, and carries a spread-sensors-watch-cloud monotony risk.

## The Curator's Cuts

24 candidates were generated; the curator selected 17 for the field. Curator's rationale for the cut:

> Selected 17 for maximum spread of verbs (bluff-pricing, pruning, sequencing, undo, wishing, negotiating, breeding, forging, arranging, curating memory) while covering competitive, solo, cozy, and the set's only fully co-op entry (N23), and keeping every kit hard-exercised across the slate. The 7 cuts are duplicate-driven: N14 collides head-on with already-qualified seed S3 (same name PRECEDENT, same LLM-adjudication territory). N11 is a near-twin of N13 — both are manor-stealth over rendered ISMCTS particle beliefs; N13's posterior-as-health-bar is the purer, lower-scope version, while N11 leans hardest on a Ninja Tanks reskin (novelty-vs-lineage risk). N7 is the third economic-bluffing duel; N1 already owns deception-as-economics with the fresher judge-priced-cover-story mechanic, and N7's remaining parts (feints, decay, multiplicative score) are all covered elsewhere. N2 and N6 share the commission-then-lab-certify loop; N6's genome-as-AST recombination is the bigger leap, whereas N2 reads as "Lution with a lab stamp." N24 was the third commission-pipeline game and the second wish-granting compiler (after N15). N21's hidden-heritable-trait breeding duplicates N6's core and its belief-hint system duplicates N3/N23. N20 self-describes as a LUMENGRID reskin ("lasers to ladles") — weakest against the novelty lens — and the cozy lane was oversubscribed at nine candidates, so the three weakest cozy overlaps (N20, N21, N24) were trimmed while retaining six diverse family options (N3, N10, N17, N19, N22, N23).

The 7 concepts cut before the tournament:

### THE PROVING FLOOR `[N2]` *(platform-maximalist lens)*

**THE PROVING FLOOR** — an auto-battler whose expansions are born inside the game.
- **Pitch**: You commission new parts, an agent codes them, and the balance lab — diegetic, on-screen, thousands of bouts — certifies or rejects them before they may enter anyone's shop.
- **Core loop**: Draft parts, assemble a secret loadout, watch a deterministic seeded battle replay. Between matches, spend winnings to commission a part concept, then watch its certification run live and see it stamped ADMITTED (with its measured win-band) or REJECTED into a public graveyard that maps the design space's edges.
- **Signature mechanic**: Empirical admission — a part exists only if the lab proves it lands in the 45–55% band across archetype matchups. Certification reports are player-facing spectacle, not dev tooling.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/llm implement-jobs (guarded, test-gated) code parts against the effect-AST catalog. @deev/lab is the shipping mechanic: byte-identical certification reports with Wilson CIs shown in-game. @deev/content keeps drift-tested canonical fixtures for every admitted part. @deev/ai ISMCTS drafts against your hidden loadout. @deev/core makes battles pure replay.
- **Lineage & leap**: Succeeds Lution's grow-your-own content and registry land-grab; the leap is moving the admissions authority from golden tests to empirical proof — the measurement instrument becomes the fantasy.
- **Session shape**: One draft–battle–commission cycle ≈ 12 minutes; certification jobs finish while you're away, Leyline-style, with a welcome-back verdict.

### SPOILS OF THE TIDE `[N7]` *(catalog-remix lens)*

**SPOILS OF THE TIDE** — an auction house where every lot is rotting on the block, and half the bids in the room are lies.
- **Pitch**: A dockside fish-and-relic auction where goods have half-lives and bids have doubles. You bluff in an economy, not a battlefield.
- **Core loop**: Each in-game day a procedurally generated catch lands. Players slot bids face-down — real bids and feint bids rendered identically to opponents. Counter-bidding keeps your turn (FORGE's tempo refund). Call a suspected feint and the feinter must pay a settlement or eat the decaying lot. Won goods rot in your hold with condition-dependent half-lives; you race contracts against spoilage. Score is gold × reputation — dumping spoiled stock tanks reputation, so greed can't win alone.
- **Signature mechanic**: Feint-bid duality — Ninja Tanks' decoy/real indistinguishability transplanted into an auction, where every bluff carries a decay clock. Chicken, priced in rot.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/ai ISMCTS over hidden bid-truthfulness beliefs; @deev/lab proves a feint-rate equilibrium exists (Wilson CIs on call/fold strategies); @deev/content verifier guarantees every generated day's catch is survivable; @deev/core transcripts replay whole market days; @deev/ui tap-confirm bidding on mobile.
- **Lineage & leap**: FORGE's counter-bid auctions + Ninja Tanks' feints + Halflight's decay + Mythgarden's multiplicative score. The leap: bluffing with zero combat — deception as pure economics.
- **Session shape**: One market day is 10–12 minutes; save envelope closes between days, so interruption is free.

### MIRRORTHIEF `[N11]` *(catalog-remix lens)*

**MIRRORTHIEF** — three identical thieves enter the mansion; only one of them is you.
- **Pitch**: A heist duel where your decoys are bound by the same truth-in-advertising as your real body. Bluffing under honest telegraphs.
- **Core loop**: You script two clockwork doubles and pilot one true thief through a guard-patrolled manor, racing a rival (human or AI) doing the same. Every body — real or decoy — honestly telegraphs its next action one turn ahead (one function drives display and execution, MMS-style). Guards and the rival see all six bodies rendered identically. The rival wins big by tagging your true body; you score loot × silence, multiplicative, so tripping alarms to move faster erodes the other axis.
- **Signature mechanic**: Honest telegraphs applied to your own lies. Ninja Tanks let feints merely look real; here decoys must behave plausibly under a no-lying rule — deception becomes choreography, planned a turn ahead, not painted on.
- **Platform exploitation**: The flagship @deev/ai ISMCTS showcase: belief particles over which body is real, re-determinized each turn. @deev/lab runs the killer invariant — decoys must be statistically indistinguishable from true bodies under bot play, with CIs — turning "is the bluff viable?" into a measured claim. @deev/content schemas manors with a solvable-not-pre-solved verifier; @deev/core deterministic replay for post-heist reveals.
- **Lineage & leap**: Ninja Tanks' decoy duality + Oracle's hidden intent + MMS's honest telegraph, deliberately collided. The leap: the telegraph constrains the bluffer, not the boss.
- **Session shape**: One manor floor is 10–15 minutes, mobile portrait, save envelope per floor.

### PRECEDENT `[N14]` *(radical-form lens)*

**PRECEDENT** — a roguelike governed by your own case law
- **Pitch**: A dungeon crawler whose rulebook grows from your rulings. When the engine hits an ambiguous interaction, you go to Hearing; your verdict becomes compiled, binding law for every future run.
- **Core loop**: Runs are 15-minute crawls seeded with deliberately underspecified collisions (fire spell vs ice golem: melt, douse, or shatter?). At each unruled collision, play pauses; you rule in one sentence; @deev/llm compiles the ruling into an effect-AST amendment, golden-tested, appended to your Codex forever. Future runs — and future enemies — exploit your case law against you.
- **Signature mechanic**: The precedent engine. Nomic made rule-change a move; here rulings *compile*, and old run transcripts are re-executed against the new Codex to surface contradictions you must resolve on Appeal — jurisprudence as endgame content.
- **Platform exploitation**: The llm kit's implement-jobs and effect-AST catalog are the spine; @deev/content golden fixtures gate every amendment; @deev/core replay convergence literally powers Appeals; @deev/lab watches whether your Codex drifts toward degenerate player-favoring law and arms a difficulty governor.
- **Lineage & leap**: Lution's never-closing content space and registry land-grab — but the growing artifact is the *rules*, not cards, and it's single-player. The discovery phase is your own legal system's emergent loopholes.
- **Session shape**: One run is 10–20 minutes; Hearings are natural put-it-down points; Codex lives in a versioned save envelope.

### Ember & Thyme `[N20]` *(cozy-family lens)*

**EMBER & THYME** — evocative name
- **Pitch**: A family-run hearth inn where cooking is exact-match flavor chemistry — and the whole village's palate slowly learns from everything you serve.
- **Core loop**: Mornings, forage and shop (kid-friendly ingredient matching). Evenings, service: guests arrive with palate targets expressed as flavor-bit profiles; you compose dishes from ingredients whose flavor bits shift as they ripen or wilt. Over-seasoning fails outright, so surplus flavor must be diverted into stocks and preserves rather than plated.
- **Signature mechanic**: Taste-memory. Every dish served nudges that guest's future palate target along a deterministic drift rule — the family is collectively *training the village's taste*, opening or closing future recipe space. The discovery game is steering the meta-palate toward combinations you can actually source next season.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/content is load-bearing: ingredient/guest/recipe zod schemas, canonical fixtures with drift tests, and the explicit-seeds verifier — every generated market week must admit full service (`solvable`) without single-dish spam (`notPreSolved`). @deev/lab bots simulate whole seasons to prove palate drift can't corner a village into unsatisfiability (invariant aborts, Wilson CIs). @deev/core seeds ripening; @deev/ui's tap-select-then-confirm handles plating.
- **Lineage & leap**: LUMENGRID's exact-match bitmasks and excess-as-hazard, re-skinned from lasers to ladles; Halflight's condition-dependent change without its shop/decay-mana center. The leap: a content space that drifts under your own play — Lution's never-closing space achieved with zero LLM.
- **Session shape**: One dinner service is 10–15 minutes; parallel play splits naturally — one player preps, one plates. Save envelopes hold mid-service.

### Rockpool `[N21]` *(cozy-family lens)*

**ROCKPOOL** — evocative name
- **Pitch**: A windowsill tide-pool terrarium whose creatures genuinely evolve. Each family member stewards a species; after a month, your family's pool is unrepeatable.
- **Core loop**: A daily ten-minute tend: adjust light, salinity, and current; place shelters; choose which pairings breed. Traits are hidden and heritable, but every trait must express as an observable behavior — you learn your creatures by watching, and log guesses in a shared field notebook the game quietly scores.
- **Signature mechanic**: Legible genetics. Genomes are hidden but deterministic and seeded, under an honest-telegraph rule applied to biology: nothing invisible ever matters. Discovery is the family designing breeding experiments together — the kid notices "the stripey ones hide at noon," the parent isolates the allele.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/lab runs headless ecology sweeps proving no parameter corner triggers silent extinction cascades (invariant aborts), with Wilson CIs on trait-fixation rates. @deev/ai's particle-belief machinery is repurposed as the in-game "naturalist" hint system — its beliefs over hidden genomes are a viewable AI-transparency surface. @deev/content holds species/trait schemas with drift tests; @deev/core's save envelopes and determinism carry a weeks-long pool, with Leyline-style away simulation.
- **Lineage & leap**: Leyline's idle care, MMS's honest telegraphs, Muju's belief tracking — aimed at biology instead of war. The leap: the player becomes the scientist, and the portfolio's hidden-info AI becomes a family hypothesis engine.
- **Session shape**: Daily tends of 10 minutes; generations resolve at commit points; away simulation with a welcome-back summary makes interruption the norm, not the exception.

### The Wishing Workshop `[N24]` *(cozy-family lens)*

**THE WISHING WORKSHOP** — evocative name
- **Pitch**: A cozy automaton-repair shop where kids commission toys that don't exist yet — and Claude actually builds them into the game overnight.
- **Core loop**: Days at the bench: broken windup animals arrive as small machine puzzles — behavior graphs and gear routes to restore. Evenings, the family writes a wish ("a duck that paints rainbows on the pond") and the workshop's spirit codes it into a real automaton by morning. It arrives *broken by design*, becomes tomorrow's puzzle, then joins the shop's village display doing visible work.
- **Signature mechanic**: Commission-then-repair. The family authors the content, but the LLM delivers it deliberately broken, so a child's own wish becomes the next puzzle. The content space never closes because your kids keep wishing.
- **Platform exploitation**: @deev/llm at full stretch: guarded implement-jobs writing against an effect-AST catalog of automaton behaviors, retry-with-feedback, per-toy golden tests — Lution's pipeline aimed at a cozy audience. @deev/content's verifier proves every generated toy repairable-but-not-prerepaired (`solvable`/`notPreSolved`). @deev/core keeps repairs deterministic and replayable; @deev/ui's tap-select-then-confirm keeps small fingers safe.
- **Lineage & leap**: Lution's self-growing content space pointed at co-creation instead of competition; LUMENGRID's machine-verified puzzles. The leap: kids as commissioning designers — the extinction engine replaced by a growing shelf of family-authored toys.
- **Session shape**: One repair is a 10-minute unit with commit-point undo; commissions resolve asynchronously overnight, which makes interruption structural. Mobile-first.
